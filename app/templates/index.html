🗳️ WHAT IS VOTING?

The Power of Your Vote

🗳️

What is Voting?

Voting is the democratic right of every eligible Indian citizen to elect their representatives to the Lok Sabha, State Assemblies, and local bodies. It is the most direct form of civic participation in India's parliamentary democracy.

Fundamental Right
⚖️

Constitutional Basis

Article 326 of the Indian Constitution grants universal adult suffrage. Every citizen who meets the eligibility criteria has an equal, undivided vote — regardless of caste, religion, gender, or economic status.

Article 326
🏛️

Types of Elections

  • 🏛️ Lok Sabha — Every 5 years (National)
  • 🏢 Vidhan Sabha — State Legislature
  • 🏘️ Local Body — Panchayat & Municipal
  • 👤 Rajya Sabha — By elected MLAs
✅ VOTER ELIGIBILITY IN INDIA

Who Can Vote?

✅ You ARE Eligible If:

  • Age: 18 years or older on the qualifying date
  • Citizen: Indian citizen by birth, descent, or registration
  • Residence: Ordinarily resident in a constituency
  • Enrolled: Name appears in the Electoral Roll
  • Sound mind: Not declared of unsound mind by a court

❌ You CANNOT Vote If:

  • Under 18 years of age on the qualifying date
  • Non-citizen or foreign national
  • Convicted and sentenced to 2+ years imprisonment
  • Declared of unsound mind by a competent court
  • Disqualified under Representation of People Act, 1950

💡 WHY VOTE?

Benefits of Exercising Your Vote

🏛️

Government Accountability

Elected officials are accountable to voters. Your vote decides who governs, making politicians answer to the public.

🌱

Policy That Reflects You

From healthcare to education, the policies enacted directly affect your daily life. Voting shapes these decisions.

⚖️

Equality of Voice

Every vote counts equally. A daily wage worker and a billionaire each get exactly one vote — true democratic equality.

🛡️

Rights Protection

Elected bodies pass laws protecting your fundamental rights. Non-voters give up their say in who protects those rights.

📈

Economic Development

Local and national budgets are controlled by elected governments. Your vote decides investment in roads, hospitals, and schools.

🌐

National Identity

India is the world's largest democracy. Every vote cast is a contribution to this historic democratic experiment.

📋 ELECTION PROCESS

How an Indian Election Works

1

Model Code of Conduct

ECI announces election dates; MCC comes into force immediately, restricting party activities.

2

Nomination

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer within the prescribed date.

3

Scrutiny

RO scrutinizes nominations for validity. Candidates can withdraw within a specified period.

4

Campaign Period

48 hours before polling, all campaigning must stop (Silent Period).

5

Polling Day

Voters cast ballots via EVM at their designated polling booth. VVPAT slip confirms vote.

6

Counting & Result

Votes are counted under strict supervision. Winning candidate declared by Returning Officer.

🇮🇳 PRIME MINISTERS OF INDIA SINCE 1947

All Prime Ministers who served India from Independence to today

1st PM
Jawaharlal Nehru
1947 – 1964  ·  INC

17 years in office — the longest. Founded IITs, AIIMS, IIMs & ISRO. Architect of Five-Year Plans, mixed-economy model & Non-Aligned Movement. Panchsheel doctrine. Shaped modern India's democratic foundation.

Acting PM
Gulzarilal Nanda
1964 & 1966  ·  INC

Served twice as Acting PM after the deaths of Nehru (1964) and Shastri (1966). Senior Congress leader and a champion of labour rights and social welfare.

2nd PM
Lal Bahadur Shastri
1964 – 1966  ·  INC

Led India to victory in the 1965 Indo-Pakistan War. Coined the slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan". Spearheaded the Green Revolution & White Revolution (Operation Flood). Died in Tashkent, 1966.

3rd & 6th PM
Indira Gandhi
1966–77, 1980–84  ·  INC

India's only female PM. Led 1971 war — creation of Bangladesh. Pokhran-I nuclear test 1974. Nationalised 14 banks. Imposed Emergency (1975–77). Abolished privy purses. Assassinated in 1984.

4th PM
Morarji Desai
1977 – 1979  ·  Janata Party

First non-Congress PM of India. Led Janata Party government post-Emergency. Rolled back 42nd Amendment excesses. Restored civil liberties. Awarded Pakistan's Nishan-e-Pakistan.

5th PM
Charan Singh
1979 – 1980  ·  Janata Party (S)

Champion of farmers and agrarian India. Never faced Parliament as PM — resigned before confidence vote. Shortest-serving elected PM. Advocated for rural India's economic rights throughout his career.

7th PM
Rajiv Gandhi
1984 – 1989  ·  INC

Youngest PM at age 40. Launched India's IT revolution, computerised Railways & postal services. Lowered voting age from 21→18. Indo-Sri Lanka Accord. Navratna PSUs. Assassinated in 1991.

8th PM
V. P. Singh
1989 – 1990  ·  Janata Dal

Implemented Mandal Commission recommendations — 27% OBC reservations in government jobs. Led National Front minority government. Resigned after Lok Sabha confidence vote was lost.

9th PM
Chandra Shekhar
1990 – 1991  ·  SJP

Led Samajwadi Janata Party. Minority government backed by Congress. Managed 1991 Balance of Payments crisis by pledging gold reserves. Served only 7 months.

10th PM
P. V. Narasimha Rao
1991 – 1996  ·  INC

Father of India's economic reforms. Launched LPG (Liberalisation, Privatisation, Globalisation) with FM Manmohan Singh. Look East Policy. Nuclear deterrence advances. Managed Babri Masjid aftermath.

11th PM
H. D. Deve Gowda
1996 – 1997  ·  Janata Dal

JD leader from Karnataka. Led United Front coalition government. Focused on rural development and federalism. Resigned after Congress withdrew support from outside.

12th PM
I. K. Gujral
1997 – 1998  ·  Janata Dal

Gujral Doctrine — India gives without reciprocity to smaller neighbours. Veteran foreign minister & diplomat. Led minority government; resigned after Jain Commission report cited Congress link.

13th PM
Atal Bihari Vajpayee
1998 – 2004  ·  BJP

First non-Congress PM to complete a full 5-year term. Pokhran-II nuclear tests 1998. Won Kargil War. Golden Quadrilateral highway network. PMGSY rural roads. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan. Bharat Ratna 2015.

14th PM
Manmohan Singh
2004 – 2014  ·  INC

First Sikh PM. Oxford & Cambridge economist. NREGA, RTI Act, Aadhaar, US-India Nuclear Deal (123 Agreement), Bharat Nirman. Architect of 1991 LPG reforms as Finance Minister.

15th PM
Narendra Modi Current
2014 – Present  ·  BJP

Former Gujarat CM (2001–14). First PM born after Independence. Jan Dhan, Make in India, Swachh Bharat, GST, Chandrayaan-3, Article 370 revocation, ₹78L cr infra spend. Historic third consecutive term 2024.
